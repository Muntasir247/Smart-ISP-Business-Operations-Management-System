Overview

Bangladesh ISP departmental exit principles

This page is written as a policy sheet, not a grid. It reflects common ISP practices in Bangladesh for resignation, service handover, support continuity, customer protection, and company asset control.

Confidentiality Service continuity Clearance required

Purpose. To define how a resigning employee from an ISP department must complete notice, handover, access revocation, device return, financial settlement, and customer service transition in a professional and auditable way.

Scope. Applicable to all departments commonly found in ISP companies in Bangladesh, including NOC, network field operations, customer support, billing, sales, accounts, HR, IT, and administration.

Policy tone. The rules below are written in a practical internal style used by local service providers, so supervisors can apply them directly without converting them into table rows.

Core compliance principles

  1. Every resignation must be submitted in writing or through an approved internal system.
  2. No employee may leave with active access, unpaid dues, or unreturned company property.
  3. Department heads must ensure customer tickets, POP work, routes, and service calls are handed over before the last working day.
  4. Company records, configuration files, passwords, customer data, and network credentials remain strictly confidential.
Rules

Detailed resignation and departmental conduct rules

These clauses are arranged in a readable policy format to look and feel like a printed handbook page.

1. Notice and resignation submission

  1. Permanent employees should normally give 30 days' notice; senior technical, NOC, and managerial roles may require 60 to 90 days depending on the appointment letter.
  2. Contractual, project-based, or probationary staff may follow the notice period written in the contract, but the department may extend handover time if service continuity is at risk.
  3. Resignation letters must mention the last working date, reason for leaving, and confirmation that pending work will be handed over properly.
  4. The employee must not stop reporting duties until the resignation is formally acknowledged by HR or management.

2. Departmental clearance and handover

  1. Before release, the employee must hand over all ongoing tasks, service records, escalation notes, customer complaints, and vendor communication.
  2. NOC and field staff must document network topology notes, link status, device locations, credentials handled under duty, and current fault status.
  3. Customer support staff must transfer active tickets, complaint history, call notes, and unresolved billing disputes.
  4. Billing, accounts, and sales teams must close open entries, hand over receivable status, and return all company documentation.

3. Access control and information security

  1. All email, CRM, billing, router, ONU/ONT, support portal, WhatsApp Business, and admin panel access must be revoked before or on the final working day.
  2. Password sharing, credential copying, and unauthorized data export are strictly prohibited.
  3. Employees may not retain customer lists, network maps, IP plans, configuration backups, or internal pricing sheets after exit.
  4. If a security risk is suspected, the IT or NOC team may change shared passwords immediately and document the action.

4. Company assets and property return

  1. All company property must be returned in usable condition, including laptop, desktop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset, SIM card, attendance device, ID card, modem, router, tools, uniforms, and keys.
  2. Any lost, damaged, or missing item may be charged to the employee according to the asset register and approval of management.
  3. Field technicians must return splicing tools, testing equipment, ladders, safety items, and network access devices.
  4. No employee may clear exit until the asset return form is signed by Admin or the asset custodian.

5. Final financial settlement

  1. Final settlement should include salary up to the last working day, approved overtime if applicable, leave encashment if allowed, and any deductions listed in the employment contract.
  2. Advance salary, training cost recovery, device replacement cost, or notice buyout may be adjusted where lawful and approved by management.
  3. Reimbursement claims submitted before exit must be verified by Finance or Accounts before payment.
  4. Settlement is usually completed after clearance, but the timeline may extend if audit, asset recovery, or dues verification is pending.

6. Professional conduct during notice period

  1. The resigning employee must maintain regular attendance, punctuality, and proper behavior until release.
  2. Work handover should remain cooperative; no intentional delay, misguidance, or data withholding is allowed.
  3. Customer calls, technical escalations, and field issues must be handled responsibly during the transition period.
  4. Managers may reassign duties, shorten access rights, or change shift allocation during notice if operational risk exists.

7. Leave, absence, and notice protection

  1. Employees on resignation notice may take leave only with approval from the reporting manager and HR.
  2. Unauthorized absence, repeated late arrival, or leaving the station without handover may result in disciplinary action or extended notice.
  3. If urgent service incidents occur, the department may require the employee to remain available for clarification during the transition.
  4. All attendance irregularities must be settled before clearance is finalized.

8. Customer service and service continuity

  1. Customer experience must not drop during an employee exit; all active complaints and escalations must be transferred properly.
  2. For field and network staff, pending outages, link repairs, PoP issues, and maintenance windows must be documented with a replacement contact.
  3. For sales or account teams, active proposals, renewal follow-ups, and collection notes must be handed over to the next responsible person.
  4. Any critical incident handled by the resigning employee must be summarized in a handover note.

9. Confidentiality and non-disclosure

  1. Employees must not disclose client databases, bandwidth plans, pricing policies, tower or fiber route details, internal meeting notes, or compensation data.
  2. Any external communication about company operations must be approved through management if it uses internal information.
  3. After exit, former employees should not misuse company branding, service credentials, or private technical records.
  4. Breaches may result in legal action or recovery claims under applicable Bangladeshi laws and the employment contract.

10. Exit interview and recommendation handling

  1. An exit interview should be completed for feedback, experience review, and operational lessons learned.
  2. The company may decline recommendation issuance until all clearance steps are complete.
  3. Positive recommendation letters may be issued for employees who leave with proper notice, clean clearance, and good performance history.
  4. Any adverse conduct, asset loss, or policy breach may be recorded in the exit file.
Department notes

Common ISP department expectations in Bangladesh

These expectations reflect operational reality across local ISP companies and can be used as working rules for each team.

NOC / Network Operations

  • Keep port maps, OLT/ONU notes, route alarms, and emergency contacts updated.
  • Hand over router credentials, link vendor contacts, and ongoing outage logs.
  • Document any fiber cut, power issue, or uplink dependency before resignation closes.

Field / Technical Team

  • Return tools, ladders, safety items, and work devices.
  • Close pending installation, relocation, and troubleshooting jobs.
  • Transfer customer site notes, cable paths, and rack-room access information.

Customer Support / Call Center

  • Hand over ticket backlog, complaint status, and follow-up commitments.
  • Ensure no service ticket is left without owner assignment.
  • Remove access to call logs, CRM, and support scripts when clearance starts.

Billing / Accounts / Sales

  • Transfer outstanding collections, client dues, and invoice notes.
  • Close cash handling, receipt books, and branch records.
  • Confirm that pricing sheets, proposals, and customer account files are returned.

Important note

This document is a practical internal policy draft for an ISP company in Bangladesh. Final notice period, settlement rules, disciplinary action, and deductions should always follow the employee contract, company HR policy, and applicable Bangladeshi labor compliance requirements.
